 Project Overview

This project was done as part of the Digital Literacy course, where I took on the role of a Student Digital Ambassador. The main idea behind this project was not just to use digital tools, but to understand how to use them properly, responsibly, and in a professional way.

The project includes five tasks that cover different areas such as creating awareness about digital literacy, building an online presence, using collaboration platforms, writing professional emails, and understanding cyber safety. While working on this, I realized that being active online is very different from being aware and responsible.

This project helped me reflect on my own habits and understand how small actions in the digital world can have a long term impact.



Task Overview
🔹 Task 1 Digital Literacy Infographic

For this task, I created a one page infographic explaining what digital literacy means, why it is important, and how students can stay safe online. This helped me understand how to present information in a simple and visual way.

🔹 Task 2 Digital Portfolio

I created profiles on LinkedIn, GitHub, and Kaggle. Initially, I just thought of them as platforms to create accounts on, but while setting them up, I understood how they actually represent a student’s skills and growth over time.

🔹 Task 3 Platforms and Collaboration

I solved a beginner level problem on HackerRank and created a Google Form quiz. This was interesting because it combined both technical practice and practical use of tools for collecting responses and working with data.

🔹 Task 4 Email Etiquette

In this task, I wrote two professional emails and created a checklist for social media usage. It made me realize how important tone and structure are in communication, especially in formal situations.

🔹 Task 5 Cybercrime Awareness

I created a case study on phishing and a prevention checklist. This task made me more aware of how easily people can fall into online scams and how important it is to stay careful.
