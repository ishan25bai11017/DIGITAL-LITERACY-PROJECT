Case Study on Phishing

Phishing is a type of cybercrime where attackers try to trick people into sharing sensitive information such as passwords, bank details, or personal data. It usually happens through emails, messages, or fake websites that appear genuine.

A common example is when a student receives an email that looks like it is from a well known company offering an internship. The email appears professional and creates urgency by saying the offer will expire soon. It includes a link to verify details. When the student clicks the link, they are redirected to a fake website that looks very similar to the original one. The form then asks for login credentials or personal information. Once the details are entered, the attacker gains access to the student’s account.

Phishing mainly targets students, job seekers, and people who are actively looking for opportunities. These individuals are more likely to trust such messages and respond quickly.

The consequences can be serious. The victim may lose access to their accounts, face data theft, or even financial loss. It can also lead to stress and loss of trust in online platforms.

This case shows that even small actions like clicking a link without checking can cause major problems. Being careful and verifying sources is very important.
